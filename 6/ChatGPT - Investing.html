You said:
what is spirulina?
ChatGPT said:

Spirulina is a blue-green algae (technically a cyanobacterium) that people use as a nutrient-dense supplement

Heres the simple breakdown:

What it is

  • A microscopic algae that grows naturally in alkaline lakes

  • Used as food for hundreds of years (Aztecs were into it)

Whats inside (why people take it)

Spirulina is famous because its crazy nutrient-packed:

  • ~6070% protein (complete protein with all essential amino acids)

  • Iron (plant-based, pretty bioavailable)

  • B vitamins (B1, B2, B3)

  • Iodine & magnesium (varies by source)

  • Phycocyanin a powerful antioxidant (also what gives it the blue color)

  • Chlorophyll

Common benefits people look for

  • Supports energy & endurance

  • Helps with immune function

  • Antioxidant & anti-inflammatory effects

  • May help with cholesterol & blood sugar balance

  • Popular in detox / gut health routines

How people consume it

  • Powder (mixed into smoothies, juice, water)

  • Tablets or capsules

  • Sometimes added to protein bars, drinks, or health foods

Heads-up: it has a strong earthy / seaweed taste, so smoothies help.

Safety notes

  • Quality matters a lot poor sources can contain heavy metals or toxins

  • People with autoimmune diseases, phenylketonuria (PKU), or on immunosuppressants should be careful

Spirulina vs Chlorella (quick compare)

  • Spirulina more protein & antioxidants

  • Chlorella better for binding heavy metals (cell wall)
